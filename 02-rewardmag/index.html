Classic RL models — where does reward magnitude matter?

Task: 2-arm deterministic bandit. Arm 0 → reward R; arm 1 → 0.

Core question: Does increasing R (while holding α fixed) speed up learning — i.e., shorten the trials needed to reach P(correct) ≥ 90%?
The answer depends entirely on where R appears in the update rule or policy. Plots show reinforcements to criterion vs reward size (0.1 → 1.0); red ticks = mean ± SEM.
